About

I am a Ph.D. student in Information Science (Data Science concentration) at the University of North Texas, affiliated with the Intelligent Data Engineering and Analytics (IDEA) Lab (Prof. Haihua Chen), where I also serve as Lab Manager, and the Metacognitive Instructional Regulation Agents (MIRA) Lab (Prof. Ji Hyun Yu). I currently hold a Teaching Fellow position at UNT.

My research addresses a core challenge in AI-era education: as learners engage with complex, ill-structured problems, particularly in data science contexts where AI tools are increasingly embedded in the workflow, the cognitive and metacognitive processes that drive learning remain largely invisible. I study how these processes, including metacognitive regulation and problem-solving strategies, can be made legible through computational analysis of learner data.

Methodologically, I take a multi-method approach. For scalable analysis, I draw on NLP and data mining techniques applied to learner text, including discussion posts, think-aloud protocols, and reflective writing, to automatically identify patterns in cognition and regulation.

These two strands converge in an applied direction: the design and development of intelligent agents and learning platforms that can detect, respond to, and scaffold metacognitive regulation in real time. Earlier work on cognitive presence and Community of Inquiry in MOOC contexts laid the empirical foundation for this trajectory and informs how I think about automated detection of learning processes at scale.
