Course Description

This course introduces students to the basic concepts and components of Information Architecture (IA) within the context of end-user and organizational needs. It provides students with an understanding of the intellectual technologies necessary to design and implement effective and cost-efficient information systems — including digital libraries, database systems, web-accessible resources, and collaborative computer systems.

Students conduct a collaborative term project to design and implement a real-world system integrating knowledge and skills in organization of information, visual design, human interface, and usability.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the major concepts and principles of IA — key terms, components, project development, and strategy in professional settings.
  • Develop a holistic view of IA including design, research, prototyping, usability testing, and evaluation.
  • Apply user-centered design methodology and document the design thinking process for IA projects.
  • Demonstrate mastery of IA research methods (e.g., Card Sorting, User Interviews) and consult professional resources for IA research and strategy.
  • Create IA documentation (e.g., User Persona, Use Cases) and utilize IA implementation tools.
  • Develop and test a web-based application/information system following IA best practices.

Required Textbooks

Final Project — Portfolio

Students may work individually or as a team of 2 to create a mid-fidelity prototype in Figma and present it as a web design portfolio. The project can be a web-based content management system, e-commerce website, or topic-based website — either a redesign or built from scratch.

  • Deliverable 1: Project Plan
  • Deliverable 2: Design and Prototyping with Figma
  • Deliverable 3: Portfolio Presentation (due Jul 24 at 11:59 PM CT)

Each team member must demonstrate individual contribution at each stage. Grades include both individual and team components. Use of generative AI to produce deliverables will result in 0 points.

Course Policies

Late Work

Each student has a one-time excuse for late submission of individual work. Any additional late work receives a zero automatically. Group work and final project deliverables are not accepted after the due date under any circumstances.

Resubmission / Correction

Students have a one-time opportunity to make corrections to individual work (does not apply to Module Discussions, Project Chats, or Quizzes). Contact the instructor via Canvas within 48 hours of grade release. Resubmission does not guarantee a higher grade.

Communication & Feedback

Use Canvas Messages for all course communication. The instructor responds Tue & Thu 9:00–11:00 AM. Assignment grades and feedback are provided within one week of the due date.

Discussions

Original posts must be submitted by Thursday midnight; replies to classmates by Sunday 11:59 PM CT. Each post should be 200–250 words with APA 7th edition citations. Students must post their original before seeing replies.

Academic Integrity

Generative AI use on assignments, discussions, or the final project results in 0 points. Per UNT Policy 06.003 and IS guidelines: First offense — zero on the assignment; Second offense — F for the course. See IS departmental guidelines.
