Course Description

This course introduces students to data analysis, data mining, text mining, and knowledge discovery principles, concepts, theories, and practices. It is designed for aspiring or practicing information professionals and covers the basics of working with data from a hands-on and practical perspective.

Classes incorporate lectures, discussion, the practice of learned concepts, and readings. Students will learn how to approach data and data mining tasks using Python, and will gain foundational knowledge of data mining and text mining techniques and their business applications.

Course Objectives

  • Participate actively in class and community discussions by analyzing, constructing, and evaluating information from textbooks, readings, and course activities.
  • Understand the fundamental principles, concepts, theories, and practices of data analysis, data mining, and knowledge discovery.
  • Apply acquired knowledge to solve real-world problems using provided datasets and construct bibliographies from scholarly sources.
  • Write a high-quality data analysis report that demonstrates mastery of course content and professional communication standards.

Required Textbook

Course Policies

Late Work

Each student has a one-time excuse for late submission. Any late work after this excuse will receive a zero automatically. Group work and final project deliverables will not be accepted after the due date under any circumstances. If you have a serious illness or emergency, contact the instructor immediately.

Communication

Use Canvas Messages for all course-related communication — responses are faster than email. Assignment grades and feedback will be provided within one week of the due date.

Academic Integrity

Academic dishonesty (cheating, plagiarism, fabrication, etc.) will not be tolerated. Per UNT Policy 06.003 and Department of Information Science guidelines: First offense — zero on the assignment; Second offense — grade of F for the course. See IS departmental guidelines for details.

ADA / Accessibility
